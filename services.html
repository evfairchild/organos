Organos provides a range of studies that can illuminate the dose effect of compounds on cardiac myocytes, ranging from acute safety pharmacology to chronic studies to understand long term cardiotoxicity.

Electrophysiological (EP) Testing

Organos can provide analysis of actional potential dynamics and calcium transients in our cardiac MPS using voltage sensitive dyes, a GCaMP calcium reporter, and high-resolution microscopy. Measurements can be performed at the baseline beat rate, as well as with electrically paced cells. Organos provides critical information on compound induced changes to the action potential duration, calcium upstroke dynamics, and triangulation as a function of drug dose.

Organos uses a proprietary in silico framework to computationally invert these measurements into mechanism of action, and provides estimated IC50 values of ion channel binding for hERG, Peak Nav1.5, Late Nav 1.5, and Cav 1.2. Our computational framework can also be used to project the results obtained in the MPS onto mature human phenotypes, as well as a canine cellular model to predict expected differential effects and dose dynamics across species and in adult native cells.

Muscle Twitch and Coupled EP-Twitch Dynamics

Through the use of high resolution imaging of the beating construct, changes in muscle twitch dynamics can be calculated in terms of length and magnitude of twitch strains in comparison to control chips. The upstroke and relaxation dynamics, as well as differential in observed changes in force generation compared to calcium transient can also be determined.

Chronic Exposure

Due to the stability of the cardiac MPS systems, both EP and twitch studies can be done under long term (>24 hour) compound exposure to test for chronic exposure liability. Long term analyses can be performed for as described above and can be detailed to client's needs.
